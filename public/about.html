About Resurrexi Labs

Adversarial systems research infrastructure

Mission

Resurrexi Labs investigates adversarial systems—environments where competing interests generate structural conflict—across AI safety, political economy, computational psychology, and infrastructure engineering. The unifying framework treats all domains as adversarial environments where optimal outcomes require balancing competing interests rather than eliminating conflict.

Research spans theoretical frameworks (Doctrine of Consensual Sovereignty), empirical analysis (cryptocurrency event studies, offshore finance), computational validation (trauma as maladaptive learning), and infrastructure engineering (deliberately heterogeneous distributed systems).

Research Philosophy

"Production-grade research infrastructure on deliberately heterogeneous hardware—because understanding failure modes requires systems that occasionally fail."

Frankencluster Philosophy

Building distributed systems from scavenged hardware teaches resource heterogeneity, failure domains, and storage topology in ways cloud providers abstract away. This isn't a compromise—it's a research methodology.

Core Principles

  • Heterogeneity as Feature: Mixed AMD Ryzen 9 (9900X, 5900X, HX370), Intel i7/i5, and potato-tier Celeron processors create real-world adversarial conditions. Scheduling across disparate compute exposes assumptions homogeneous clouds hide.
  • Failure as Learning: Systems that occasionally fail teach failure modes. Cloud providers abstract away the interesting parts—storage topology, network partitions, resource contention under heterogeneous load.
  • "Because I Can" as Valid Research: Cross-chassis GPU power delivery (7800 XT in PurrPower powered by SudoSenpai's PSU) started as "can we?" and became "what does this teach us about power delivery, PCIe topology, and system integration?"
  • Intentional Vulnerability: VeryVuln exists specifically to be compromised—a legitimate target for red team experiments rather than borrowed production systems.

Notable Achievements

  • Dual GPU inference (7900 XTX + 7800 XT) with cross-chassis power delivery—185W drawn from external PSU during inference
  • PCIe x8/x8 bifurcation validated via upstream port inspection (not x16/x16 as lspci initially suggested)
  • Custom StorageClass with node affinity to prevent flash drive murder via random provisioning
  • WireGuard tunnel enabling cluster access from Azerbaijan during travel

Interdisciplinary by Default: Solutions emerge from connecting disparate domains. Finance background informs security research (risk modeling = threat modeling, portfolio optimization = resource allocation). 20+ years of varied interests enable pattern recognition across disciplines.

Documentation as External Memory: Enables velocity, pre-compiles context for efficient collaboration, and makes research reproducible. Every experiment, session log, and failed attempt gets documented.

Research Program

The Adversarial Systems Research program establishes a new field investigating stability, alignment, and friction dynamics in complex systems. Each publication serves as proof-of-concept for different application domains:

  • Political Governance: Stakeholder consent vs technocratic competence (Consensual Sovereignty)
  • Financial Markets: Cryptocurrency volatility, regulatory stability vs market innovation, offshore finance as hedging
  • Human Development: Trauma as maladaptive learning from adversarial training environments
  • AI Alignment: Multi-agent systems with competing objectives, autonomous red teams

Unifying framework formalizes relationships between stakes, voice, and friction—applicable to algorithmic governance, climate negotiations, autonomous agents, and any system where consent structures remain undefined but friction dynamics are observable.

Infrastructure

7-Node Kubernetes Cluster

  • 58 total cores across AMD Ryzen 9 (3 nodes), Intel i7/i5 (3 nodes), Celeron (1 intentionally vulnerable target)
  • 168GB RAM (128GB + 32GB + 8GB main nodes)
  • 40GB VRAM (dual GPU: AMD Radeon 7900 XTX 24GB + 7800 XT 16GB)
  • ~8TB total storage with custom StorageClass for node-restricted provisioning
  • 5Gbps symmetrical fiber (Community Fibre, no CGNAT)

Network Architecture

  • GL.iNet Flint 2 primary gateway (OpenWrt, WireGuard, AdGuard)
  • WireGuard tunnel for global access (even from Azerbaijan)
  • Cloudflare Tunnel for public-facing services
  • Custom IP scheme: .99 downwards for static nodes, .100-.200 DHCP pool

Full topology documentation: resurrexi.dev/infrastructure

Name Origin

Resurrexi (Latin: "I have risen") reflects the recursive self-improvement philosophy. Systems that fail, learn, and rise stronger. Research that iterates through version-controlled preprints. Infrastructure rebuilt from failure modes.

The name also carries personal significance—emerging from extended adversarial training environments (see: Trauma as Bad Training Data) into building systems that make adversarial dynamics explicit and manageable.
