About Resurrexi Labs

Computational Research Division

Research infrastructure for adversarial systems analysis. Part of Farzulla Research.

Mission

Resurrexi Labs builds and operates distributed infrastructure for computational experiments in adversarial systems—environments where competing interests generate structural conflict. We support research across AI alignment, autonomous agents, offensive security, and large-scale computational simulations.

The unifying framework treats all domains as adversarial environments where optimal outcomes require balancing competing interests rather than eliminating conflict.

Relationship to Farzulla Research

Farzulla Research (farzulla.org) is the academic research initiative—publishing peer-reviewed papers, preprints, and formal research outputs across political economy, computational finance, and cognitive science.

Resurrexi Labs is the computational infrastructure division—building the distributed systems, autonomous agents, and experimental platforms that power Farzulla Research experiments.

Name Origin

Resurrexi (Latin: "I have risen") reflects the recursive self-improvement philosophy. Systems that fail, learn, and rise stronger. Research that iterates through version-controlled preprints. Infrastructure rebuilt from failure modes.
